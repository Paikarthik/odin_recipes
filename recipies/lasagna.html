Lasagna

Description

A hearty and satisfying homemade lasagna recipe with a meaty, made-from-scratch tomato sauce and a deliciously cheesy filling. A more traditional homemade lasagna filling would be made with ricotta, but my mom's recipe calls for a blend of small-curd cottage cheese and Parmesan. I have found none better anywhere. Serve with a leafy green salad and crusty garlic bread for a complete Italian-inspired meal.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 pound ground pork
  • 1/2 pound lean ground beef
  • 1/2 cup minced onion
  • 1 (28 ounce) can crushed tomatoes
  • 1 (8 ounce) can tomato sauce
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley, divided
  • 1 clove garlic, crushed
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon oregano
  • 1/8 teaspoon white sugar
  • 1 package lasagna noodles
  • 1 pound small curd cottage cheese
  • 3/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 3 large eggs
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 16 ounce package shredded mozzarella cheese

Steps

  1. In a large skillet, cook ground pork, ground beef, and minced onion over medium heat until browned. Drain the fat.
  2. Stir in crushed tomatoes, tomato sauce, 1 tablespoon parsley, garlic, basil, 1 teaspoon salt, oregano, and sugar. Simmer for 30 to 45 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  3. Cook lasagna noodles in a large pot of boiling salted water until al dente. Drain and rinse with cold water.
  4. In a mixing bowl, combine cottage cheese, Parmesan cheese, eggs, remaining 1 tablespoon parsley, 2 teaspoons salt, and black pepper. Mix well.
  5. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  6. Spread 1/3 of the meat sauce in the bottom of a 9x13 inch baking dish. Layer with 1/3 of the noodles, then 1/3 of the cheese mixture, and 1/3 of the shredded mozzarella. Repeat layers two more times, ending with mozzarella on top.
  7. Cover with aluminum foil and bake in the preheated oven for 25 minutes. Remove foil and bake an additional 25 minutes, or until cheese is bubbly and lightly golden.
  8. Let stand for 15 minutes before serving.
